How does AI
think?

⏱ 10 min 🎮 3 interactive demos 🤯 Includes live AI chat

In Lesson 1 you learned what AI is. Now let's go one level deeper: how does it actually work?

We're not going to talk about math or code. We're going to let you feel it — through three quick interactive demos that will make the whole thing click in about 10 minutes.

AI is a
next-word guesser

At its core, AI language models do one thing: they predict the most likely next word. Over and over and over, until an answer is complete. That's it.

Sounds simple? Try it yourself. Below is a sentence that keeps growing — your job is to pick the word you think comes next. See how close your instincts match what AI would predict.

Demo 1 — The Word Prediction Game

What word comes next?

Pick the word that feels most natural. You're thinking like an AI right now.

"The cat sat on the warm mat."

That's exactly how AI builds a sentence — one word at a time, choosing what fits best based on everything it's read.

You just did what AI does — predicted the most statistically likely next word based on context. AI has read billions of sentences, so its predictions are fast, confident, and usually right.

But here's the crucial insight: it's always predicting. AI doesn't "know" the answer — it predicts what a good answer looks like.

Why AI can be
creative or precise

If AI just picks the most likely word every time, how can it ever say something surprising or creative?

There's a hidden dial called temperature. Turn it down and AI picks the most predictable, safe word. Turn it up and it takes more risks — choosing less obvious words that can feel creative, weird, or poetic.

Drag the slider and watch the same prompt answered very differently.

💡 Why this matters for you

When you ask AI to write a professional email, you want low temperature — safe and precise. When you ask for creative ideas or brainstorming, higher temperature gives you more interesting, unexpected responses. Most AI tools let you adjust this, even if they don't call it "temperature."
