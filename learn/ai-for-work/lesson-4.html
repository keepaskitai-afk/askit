💡 The secret to good AI marketing content

Generic briefs produce generic content. The more specific your business description, the better the output. Don't just say "I'm a designer" — say "I'm a brand designer who helps B2B SaaS companies create visual identities that convert enterprise buyers." That specificity is what separates forgettable AI content from content that sounds like a real human who knows their stuff.

Making AI content
sound like you

The number one complaint about AI-generated marketing content is that it sounds generic. Here's how to fix it every time:

🎯 Use specific details, not descriptions

Don't say "I help businesses grow." Say "I helped a 3-person bakery in Leeds grow Instagram from 800 to 14,000 followers in 4 months using only organic content." Specificity is what makes AI content sound human. Generic inputs produce generic outputs.

🚫 Use negative constraints

Tell the AI what NOT to do: "No exclamation marks", "Don't use the words 'passionate' or 'dedicated'", "Don't open with a question". These exclusions push the AI past its most common patterns into something more original.

🗣️ Give it your voice

Paste 2-3 examples of content you've written that you're proud of and say: "Write in this style." AI is remarkably good at pattern-matching voice. Your personality becomes the template.

Key takeaway

Marketing is no longer the task that gets pushed to last. Your business voice, five formats, minutes not hours. The bottleneck was never creativity — it was execution.
