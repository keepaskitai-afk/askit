AI for meetings —
prep, notes
& action items

⏱ 28 min 🤖 Live Claude 📋 3 meeting tools

The average professional attends 23 hours of meetings per week. A significant chunk of that time is wasted on meetings that were poorly prepared, generated unclear outcomes, or produced notes no one ever read.

AI can transform all three phases: the preparation before, the capture during, and the cleanup after. This lesson gives you practical tools for each — and you'll use them on real meeting scenarios right now.

Phase 1: Meeting
preparation

Walking into a meeting unprepared is one of the most avoidable productivity failures. AI can prepare you for almost any meeting in under two minutes. Give it the meeting type, who's attending, and your goal — it will generate the questions you should ask, the points you need to raise, and the outcomes you should push for.

Phase 2: Capturing
notes that work

Most meeting notes are a liability, not an asset. They're either so sparse they're useless, or so long no one reads them. The trick is capturing the messy reality of what happened and then letting AI shape it into something clean and actionable.

During the meeting, just type rough notes — fragments, names, half-sentences. Don't try to make it neat. Capture who said what, any decisions made, anything that came up. Then bring it here after the meeting and turn that mess into a clean summary in seconds.

Key takeaway

Three tools, three phases. Prepare in 2 minutes. Capture messy, clean it up in 60 seconds. Follow up automatically. The whole meeting workflow costs you less time than the meeting used to start.
