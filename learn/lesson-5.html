✉️ Your Mission
Write that hard email — in the next 10 minutes
1

Open your AI tool

Go to claude.ai or chatgpt.com in a new tab. Both are free. Claude tends to write more naturally — your call.

2

Use this prompt — fill in the blanks

Copy this and replace the parts in brackets with your actual situation:

"I need to write an email to [person or organization]. The situation is: [explain what happened or what you need in 2–3 sentences]. I want the tone to be [professional / friendly / firm but polite]. Please write a clear, concise email that [specific outcome you want — e.g. requests a refund, explains the situation, asks for a meeting]."
💡 Tip: The more specific you are in the brackets, the better the result. Don't be vague — AI works best with real details.
3

Refine it until it sounds like you

Read what AI gives you. Does it sound right? Too formal? Too long? Just tell it. Try one of these follow-up messages:

"Make it shorter and more direct."
"The tone feels too formal — make it warmer."
"Add a line about [specific thing you forgot to mention]."
💡 Tip: You're the editor. AI gives you the draft — you decide when it's ready. Two or three back-and-forths is totally normal.
4

Send it

Copy the final version into your email client. Read it one last time. Make any final tweaks. Then send it. That's the win.

5

Reflect — what just happened?

Write a line or two below. How long did it take? How does it compare to writing it from scratch?

🔍 Your Mission
Get something confusing explained — finally, clearly
1

Pick your confusing thing

It can be anything — a clause in a lease, a medical term, a tax notice, a privacy policy, a news story you didn't understand. Pick something real that has been nagging at you.

2

Paste it in and use this prompt

Copy the confusing text, then open claude.ai and use this:

"Please explain the following to me in plain English, as if I have no background in this topic. Highlight anything important I should pay attention to, and let me know if there's anything I should be concerned about or act on. Here it is: [paste your text here]"
💡 Tip: You can paste entire pages of text. Claude handles long documents well. Don't worry about it being too long.
3

Ask follow-up questions until you actually get it

Don't stop at the first response. If anything is still unclear, keep going:

"Can you simplify the part about [specific section] even further?"
"What does [specific term] actually mean in simple words?"
"Is this normal, or should I be worried about this?"
💡 Tip: There are no stupid questions. AI doesn't judge. This is the best use of AI for many people — a patient explainer available 24/7.
4

Write down what you now understand

Summarise what you learned in your own words. This helps it stick — and it confirms you actually understand it.

📋 Your Mission
Turn your vague idea into a real first step
1

Name the thing you want to do

It could be starting a side project, getting healthier, learning something new, organising your finances, launching a product — anything you've been "thinking about" but haven't started.

2

Use this prompt to get unstuck

Open claude.ai and try this:

"I want to [describe your goal]. I haven't started yet because [main obstacle or reason — be honest]. I have roughly [available time per week] to work on this. Please give me: 1) A very simple first step I can complete this week, 2) Three questions I should answer before going further, and 3) One thing most people get wrong when starting this."
💡 Tip: Be honest about your obstacle. AI gives much better advice when you tell it why you're stuck — not just what you want to achieve.
3

Push back and pressure-test it

Don't just accept the first plan. Challenge it:

"That first step still feels too big — what's the smallest possible version of it?"
"What would you do differently if I only had 30 minutes a week?"
"What are the most common reasons people quit on something like this?"
4

Commit to one action

Write down the single first step you're committing to — and when you'll do it.

👤 Your Mission
Write a bio that actually sounds like you
1

Decide where this bio will live

LinkedIn profile? Email signature? Website about page? A conference speaker bio? The context changes the tone completely — so pick one specific place first.

2

Give AI the raw ingredients

Open claude.ai and use this prompt — fill it in with your real info:

"Please write a [short / medium length] bio for my [LinkedIn profile / email signature / website / other]. Here are the facts about me: [your job or role], [years of experience or background], [2–3 things you're proud of or known for], [one personal detail that makes you human — a hobby, where you're from, etc.]. Tone should be [professional but warm / conversational / confident and direct]. Do NOT make it sound generic or corporate. Make it sound like a real person wrote it."
💡 Tip: The "do not make it sound generic" instruction is important. AI defaults to bland corporate language — this fights that tendency.
3

Tune it until it's actually you

Read it out loud. Does it sound like you? Would you be embarrassed if a friend read it? Use these to fix it:

"This sounds too formal — I'd never say this. Make it more like how I'd introduce myself at a dinner party."
"Cut it down to 3 sentences. The most important 3."
"The last line feels weak. Give me 5 alternative closing lines."
💡 Tip: Reading it out loud is the best test. If you stumble on a word, AI probably wrote it — not you. Change it.
4

Paste your final version here

Write the final bio below. You'll use this in the next 24 hours — no more putting it off.
